Tips for before planting

Before planting Trees and Shrubs, prioritize hydration. Opt for cooler evening hours over midday planting.

Assess soil drainage by conducting a simple test if unsure. Avoid planting in areas with poor drainage. Ensure proper root care during planting, especially for B&B trees. Backfill holes with suitable soil or amendments. Compact soil gently to remove air pockets. Consider staking large trees for stability.

How to plant

When planting, dig a hole as deep as the container or root-ball and twice as wide if possible. Avoid creating water bowls in soil.

Carefully remove plants from containers, preserving the root system. For B&B trees, keep the burlap intact, burying it as it's biodegradable. Backfill holes with soil or amend with compost for poor soils. Ensure no air pockets or exposed roots when backfilling.

Tips for after planting

For the initial two years post-planting, ensure regular watering, even for drought-tolerant plants.

Water deeply and infrequently, adjusting frequency based on weather conditions. Evening watering is optimal, maintaining soil moisture overnight. Consider fertilizing with slow-release fertilizer annually in spring.

Our story runs depp

Outback Nursery began in 1998-1999 when our parents retired to the island from Aldergrove. They asked if we'd be interested in joining them and then purchased this heavily treed property.

We began clearing some trees and settled into country life trying our hand at raising some turkeys and pigs as well as growing some hedging and conifers. We joined the Farmer's Market.
